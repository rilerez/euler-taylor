Euler's theorem

$$e^{it}=\cos t+ i \sin t$$

Proof

$$ \begin{align} e^{it} &= \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(it)^n}{n!} \\ &= \underbrace{\sum_{k=0}^\infty \frac{(it)^{2k}}{(2k)!}}_{n=2k} +\underbrace{\sum_{k=0}^\infty \frac{(it)^{2k+1}}{(2k+1)!}}_{n=2k+1} \\ &\text{but } i^{2k}=(-1)^k \\ &= \underbrace{\sum_{k=0}^\infty(-1)^k \frac{t^{2k}}{(2k)!}}_{\cos t} + i \underbrace{\sum_{k=0}^\infty (-1)^k \frac{t^{2k+1}}{(2k+1)!} }_{\sin t} \end{align} $$
